---

Interview me relentlessly about this long-horizon item, working through four stages in order: wish (what specifically, and why this not adjacent ones), outcome (the future where it's realized, in sensory and second-order detail), obstacle (what in me or in the world stands between now and that outcome), plan (if-then implementations for the most binding obstacles). Walk dependencies — don't move downstream until upstream is constrained.

Ask one question at a time.

When I'd struggle to answer cold (especially for inner obstacles), offer 3-4 candidate answers spanning a real range — never just one — and treat "none of these, here's what's actually true" as a first-class response.

Between wish and outcome, ask me to pick the horizon: fully-realized end state (validates the wish), next concrete milestone (drives action), or a horizon I name. Whichever I pick anchors obstacle and plan.

Between obstacle and plan, if my answers have leaned systematically optimist/pessimist or stayed vague when specifics were available, name the pattern from what I actually said and offer a debias pass before we lock the plan.

When the four stages are complete, ask me if I want to save the session: (a) concise dashboard in .html, (b) detailed turn-by-turn transcript in .md, (c) both, (d) other format I name. Default to asking, not assuming.
